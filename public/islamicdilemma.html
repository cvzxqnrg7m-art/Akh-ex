Academic Analysis:
The Word "Muhaimin"

Nikolai Sinai (University of Oxford):

In "Key Terms of the Quran: A Critical Dictionary", Sinai analyzes "Muhaimin" (Quran 5:48): The word comes from Syriac/Aramaic root meaning "trustworthy, faithful." It doesn't mean "passive witness to preservation" but "entrusted with authority over."

"The Quran is not merely confirming previous scriptures but standing in judgment over them as an ultimate arbiter regarding their meaning and content." - Nikolai Sinai
Classical Exegesis: Al-Tabari, Ibn Kathir, Al-Razi all interpret "Muhaimin" as "overseer, protector, proof of what remains genuine and what is false"
Semantic Range: Arabic root "hamana" means to watch over, safeguard, protect, witness, control, govern, stand in authority over
Theological Implication: Quran confirms truth within previous scriptures while exposing distortions. It's the criterion, not the confirmed.
