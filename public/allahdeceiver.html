"And the disbelievers planned, but Allah planned. And Allah is the best of planners."
Selective Translation: Christian apologists translate "مَاكِرِينَ" as "deceivers" while ignoring its broader meaning
Dictionary Analysis: "مَكْر" (makr) means planning, designing, or strategizing. Context determines if it's positive or negative.
Consensus of Translations: Over 40 English translations of the Quran unanimously use "planner" or "schemer" in positive context

Linguistic Reality: Word Study & Context

Root Analysis: "م ك ر" (m-k-r) root meaning includes planning, designing, scheming. Like "designer" in English
خَيْرُ (Khayr) Analysis: Means "good," "charitable," "benevolent" - has positive connotations. Cannot be combined with negative "deceiver"
Contextual Usage: In Quran 3:54, Allah responds to disbelievers' evil plot against Jesus. Allah's planning is counter-planning to save Jesus
The Real Meaning
When disbelievers plotted to kill Jesus, Allah counter-planned to save him. This makes Allah the "best of planners" - the ultimate strategist who out-plans evil plans with good plans.
