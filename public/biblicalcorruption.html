The Inescapable Reality of Biblical Corruption

Bart Ehrman's scholarship reveals fundamental problems with biblical inerrancy claims:

1. No Originals: We possess zero original manuscripts - only copies made centuries later by fallible scribes.

2. Intentional Changes: Scribes altered texts for theological, political, and doctrinal reasons - not just accidental copying errors.

3. Uncertain Reconstruction: Textual criticism involves educated guesses about original readings, not certain knowledge.

4. Late Canonization: The New Testament canon wasn't fixed until the 4th century, with continued debates for centuries.

5. Evangelical Admission: Even evangelical scholars disagree on original readings, proving uncertainty exists at the highest levels.

6. Historical Context: Many "words of Jesus" reflect later church concerns rather than historical Jesus' teachings.
