Apostate Paul: Deceiver of Christianity

An examination of Paul's contradictions with Christ's teachings

Introduction

This guide organizes the key arguments presented in Felix Immanuel's work, "Apostate Paul: Deceiver of Christianity." The central thesis is that the teachings of the Apostle Paul, as found in his epistles, often contradict the direct teachings of Jesus Christ as presented in the Gospels. This guide aims to provide a comprehensive overview of these alleged discrepancies.

Double-Tongued Paul: Contradictory Teachings

Salvation

Paul presents conflicting views on whether salvation is by works (Romans 2:5-7) or by faith confession (Romans 10:9-10).

Justification by Law

Paul states that no flesh will be justified by the deeds of the law (Romans 3:20), yet also states that the doers of the law will be justified (Romans 2:13).

Law

Paul states Christ abolished the law (Ephesians 2:14-15), yet also states that through faith, they establish the law (Romans 3:31).

Conclusion: Two Different Paths

Paul's Easy Path to Pseudo-Salvation:
Paul's doctrine emphasizes salvation by grace through faith, with no works required for salvation (Ephesians 2:8-9; Romans 10:9-10). Faith itself is imputed for righteousness (Romans 4:5). This is presented as an "easy path" to salvation, often using the analogy of adoption where individuals have no active role in being adopted (Galatians 4:5).

Jesus Christ's Difficult Path to True Salvation:
In contrast, Jesus' path to salvation is described as difficult, requiring individuals to be counted worthy by God (Luke 20:35). This involves practicing righteousness that exceeds that of the Pharisees (Matthew 5:20) and being sinless to abide in God's house (John 8:34-35). Jesus' teachings emphasize drastic measures to avoid sin, including cutting off offending body parts (Matthew 18:8-9). This path is through a "narrow gate and difficult path" (Matthew 7:13-14), achieved through being "born again" of God and being without sin (John 3:5; John 8:34-36).

The Fundamental Difference:
There is a "huge" difference between Christ's and Paul's doctrines. Paul's doctrine teaches salvation by grace through faith, where obedience follows salvation and is not a prerequisite. Followers may believe they are clothed with God's righteousness by faith alone. In contrast, Christ's doctrine requires obedience as a prerequisite for salvation. Individuals must strive to be sinless and righteous to enter God's kingdom, as exemplified by Christ's life.
